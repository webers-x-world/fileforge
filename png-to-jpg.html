What is a PNG to JPG Converter?

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a lossless image format that excels at preserving transparency and sharp edges — ideal for logos, illustrations, and screenshots. JPG (Joint Photographic Experts Group), on the other hand, uses smart compression to deliver smaller file sizes, making it the format of choice for photographs and web images where storage efficiency matters.

Is the PNG to JPG conversion done in my browser?

Will converting PNG to JPG reduce quality?

JPG uses lossy compression, so some minor quality reduction is inherent to the format — this is how JPG achieves smaller file sizes. FileForge uses high-quality render settings to make the output visually indistinguishable from the original PNG for most use cases such as photos and web graphics.

What happens to the transparency in my PNG when converting to JPG?

JPG does not support transparency. When you convert a PNG with a transparent background to JPG, FileForge fills the transparent areas with a white background. This is standard behaviour across all PNG to JPG converters.
